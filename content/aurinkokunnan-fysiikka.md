title: Aurinkokunnan fysiikka (FYS2051)
---

Physics, formation theories, and observational methods of solar system bodies.

- **Period**: 3, 4
- **Content**: 12 lectures, 5 exercises, presentation, 3 essays (1/3 mandatory), programming project, home exam
- **Themes**: Theory 50%, Methods 10%, Observations 20%, Phenomena 20%
- **Teachers**: [[Anne Virkki]]

---
## Content

- Solar System objects
- Solar System formation theories
- Planetary geophysical processes (heat, radiation, petrophysics, atmospheres, oceans)
- (Planetary) radiation quantities (intensity, flux density, luminosity, magnitudes)
- Reflectivity, albedos, and integrated brightness of planetary bodies
- Observation methods (photometry, spectroscopy, polarimetry and their combinations)
- Empirical and analytical scattering laws
- HG / HG1G2 / HG12 systems
- Multiwavelength observations of Solar System objects from UV to radio
- Fundamental light scattering concepts (Stokes, scattering matrix, scattering quantities)
- Exoplanet observations
- Requirements of (extraterrestrial) life
- Spacecraft missions for planetary exploration
- Presenting scientific information in written and oral forms, reference critique
- Numerical implementation of integrated brightness for discretised spheroids
