---
title: Computational light scattering (PAP315)
---

Computational methods for electromagnetic scattering by arbitrary objects.

- **Period**: 1
- **Content**: 14 lectures, 5 exercises, 2 project tasks, home exam
- **Themes**: Theory 40%, Methods 40%, Observations 0%, Phenomena 20%
- **Teachers**: [[Karri Muinonen]], [[Antti Penttil√§]], [[Anne Virkki]], [[Yehor Surkov]], [[Zuri Gray]]

---
## Content

- Introduction to single scattering
- Scattering by a spherical particle (Mie scattering)
- Discrete-dipole approximation, volume-integral equation
- Discrete-dipole approximation, particle near a surface
- T-matrix method
- Ray optics approximation
- Introduction to multiple scattering (radiative transfer and coherent backscattering)
- Monte Carlo methods for multiple scattering
- Superposition T-matrix method
- Rough-surface scattering modeling
- Radar scattering
- Geometric optics for close-packed media
