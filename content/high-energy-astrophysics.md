---
title: High Energy Astrophysics (PAP319)
---

Emission processes, particle acceleration, and data reduction in high-energy astrophysics.

- **Period**: 1, 2
- **Content**: 12 lectures, 24 hours of lab work, grade based on labs
- **Themes**: Theory 0%, Methods 50%, Observations 50%, Phenomena 0%
- **Teachers**: [[Alexis Finoguenov]]

---
## Content

- Radiative processes
- Sources of high-energy emission and their models
- Particle acceleration
- High energy Observatories
- Galaxy clusters are high energies
- Hydrostatic Equilibrium and mass estimates
- XMM-Newton data reduction
- High energy emission from accreting black holes
- Statical tools for the analysis of high-energy spectra
- High energy data repositories and software
- Spectral simulations, and mission forecasts, proposal tools
- Quantum electrodynamic processes at high energies
- X-IFU/Athena forecasts

---
## Pre-requisites


- Basic observational astronomy
- Physics courses
