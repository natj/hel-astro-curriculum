---
title: Astrophysical light scattering problems (PAP316)
---

Application of light scattering theory to complex random media in astrophysics.

- **Period**: 4
- **Content**: 14 lectures, 3 exercises, 2 inverse methods project tasks, home exam
- **Themes**: Theory 25%, Methods 20%, Observations 25%, Phenomena 10%
- **Teachers**: [[Karri Muinonen]], [[Antti Penttilä]], [[Anne Virkki]], [[Yehor Surkov]], [[Zuri Gray]]

---
## Content

- Introduction to photometry, polarimetry, and spectrometry
- Experimental scattering measurements
- Polarimetry, photometry, and spectrometry of the Moon
- Mercury, Venus, Mars
- Asteroids
- Interplanetary dust
- Comets
- Icy moons of outer planets, Saturn’s rings
- Transneptunian objects
- Interstellar dust
- Exoplanets
